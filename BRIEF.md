# Emma's Dream House — Design Brief

## Style
- Warm, heritage-feeling, cozy
- Mid-century modern bones with soul
- Warm walnut tones, exposed beams, stone accents
- Earth tones with jewel-tone pops (emerald, amber from stained glass inspo)
- Arched doorways, leadlight/stained glass accents
- Brick exterior
- NOT sterile/modern grey — a home with character
- Pinterest board: https://pin.it/4ux8ff9OF (77 pins)

## Layout

### Basement
- Movie theatre
- Drink station / bar with stools — entertaining space
- Fun space for kids

### Floor 1 (Main)
- **Grand entrance**: Double doors, high ceiling foyer, staircase to floor 2
- **3-car garage** → leads into **mud room** → right off the **kitchen**
- **Kitchen**: Slightly separated from living, flows into dining room
- **Dining room**: Long estate table, one side is a built-in bench (bay window style but not quite)
- **Living room**: High ceilings, drop-down projector, big fireplace, large modular U-shaped couch
- **Office**: Lots of windows, bay window seating area
- **Full bathroom** on main floor
- Bay window seating in office AND possibly living room

### Floor 2
- 2 bedrooms (split from original 4 — some moved to floor 3)
- Bathroom for each bedroom (can be half bath; one full bath per floor)
- **Laundry room**

### Floor 3
- 2 more bedrooms
- Bathroom for each (same approach — full + half)
- **Master suite** (or entire floor = master suite?)
  - Walk-in closet (Emma has lots of clothes)
  - Double vanity
  - Clawfoot soaking tub
  - Large walk-in shower:
    - Two shower heads on each wall
    - Tall enough for Rob (6'4")
    - Rain shower head at top
  - Balcony with hot tub

### Outdoor
- Patio/deck area
- Grill
- Seating with umbrellas
- Lawn chairs
- Fire pit

## Key Notes
- Rob works remote — office is important
- Entertaining is important (basement + outdoor)
- Safe for kids
- Bay window seating moments throughout
- Cozy above all else
- Must feel like a HOME not a showroom
